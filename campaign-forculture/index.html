About

About

“Campaign for Culture” is a grassroots organization looking to modernize liquor laws in British Columbia. Their work involves government officials as well as the general public. To help them reach these varied groups I developed a professional, yet visually striking and accessible design language.

Identity

Identity

The direction for the identity was to combine a minimal typographic design with simple, colorful illustrations:

Logo consists of overlapping rings — representing 5 individual phases which make up the full campaign. Together with the wordmark it creates a flexible combination that works in a variety of print and digital contexts.

Warnock Pro is used to set the wordmark and all of the campaign’s print and online content. Its classic look is appropriate for official documents, while the design of its sharp serifs gives text a subtle quirkiness and a modern feel.

Illustrations

Illustrations

To augment the typography and add a colorful side to the visual language I created a series of illustrations. These were drawn to illustrate specific campaign themes and any other relevant content or information. A simple style is carried throughout, using sharp angular forms and solid colors.

Website

Website

Campaign for Culture website combines the created typography and illustrations into a stylish presentation. It is built on a simple one-column grid, and translates responsively across all screen sizes.

The site details specific issues the campaign focused on and provides a way for voters to connect with government officials regarding these topics.

The site also hosts a feed of news and articles related to the campaign and the issues it is addressing.
