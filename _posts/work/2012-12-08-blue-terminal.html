Blue Terminal is a startup developing an energy information platform. They scan policy and research publications for relevant articles and process them into a unified, searchable database. I helped their team build a user-focused research network with data-analysis and visualization tools.

{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Concept + Prototyping' %}

Early on I worked with Blue Terminal to help them name the project and develop branding that would reflect their professional approach. This resulted in a clean, editorial-style design (using Arno Pro and art-directed photography), focused on helping their fundraising efforts.

{% include system/figure_flex.html art_direction='' background='' inner_width='content_media_outerframe' figure='bluecards.jpg' caption='Business cards and responsive website gave the team a portable, on-hand set of tools they could use for meetings and project presentations.' %}

Once Blue Terminal raised sufficient funding to start development I led their team through a series of product design sessions, detailing functionality, content structure and interface direction. Specifically, we focused on what the team envisioned as key features — article feed, data-visualization tools, energy profiles and customizable user dashboards.

{% include system/figure_flex.html art_direction='left_align' inner_width='content_media_center' figure='wireframing.jpg' caption='Notes from product design sessions detailing early versions of the navigation, landing page and fuel dashboards.' %}
{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Design System' %}

Based on the range of content and functionality this project required an adaptive design system. It had to be recognizable, work across a variety of media sizes, translate across print/digital formats and be able to accommodate new content and functionality.

1. Adaptive Wordmark

Blue Terminal wordmark consists of three versions, which can be used interchangeably in order to adapt to changes in context. Their shared typographic design keeps them recognizable as part of the same family.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' background_color='gray' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='logo_wordmark.png' caption='Three versions of the wordmark adapt to variations in media or viewport size.' %}
2. Typography

Proxima Nova family is used to design the identity and set the majority of the site’s content. It is highly legible in both print and digital media, stylish, but not distracting in text and eye-catching in headings. Importantly, it provides typographic flexibility to the design system with a large range of styles and extended language support.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='content_alignto_left_margin' figure='proxima.png' caption='Bold and Regular, shown above, are two of the 42 available styles of Proxima Nova.' %}
3. Dynamic logo

While the wordmark adapts to content with interchangeable versions, Blue Terminal logo is a fully dynamic, information-rich element. Logo design reflects the informational nature of the project — combining elements of data-visualization with the project’s broad focus on energy resources.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='logo_creation.png' caption='Idea behind the logo design. The circle references Earth (symbolizing a global outlook) and acts as a container for iconography in custom logo applications.' %}

Nine rays of the logo correspond to the energy groups — geothermal, nuclear, hydroelectric, solar, wind, natural gas, coal, biofuel and crude oil. By assigning these groups to positions on the circumference of the circle (x-axis) and a quantitative variable to the length of the rays (y-axis), logo becomes dynamic when populated with data.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='dynamic_logo_range.png' caption='Assigned with energy data logo rays change length to create infinite permutations, yet remain clearly identifiable as part of one family.' %}
4. Labeling System

A variety of data can be used to set ray lengths. Examples include: energy profiles, production/consumption stats and article aggregation totals. This results in a dynamic labeling system that can provide glanceable information about the content it represents.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='dynamic_logo_samples.png' caption='A few sample label/logo visualizations. Grayed out rays are either not in focus for the topic at hand (ex. specific energy source) or present in negligible amounts.' %}
5. Iconography

Iconography plays a key role in the design language by being a major part of the labeling system and improving accessibility for foreign language users. Initially, I created a set of energy icons for the identity design.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='content_wrap' figure='iconography.png' caption='Biofuel, natural gas, nuclear, geothermal, coal, solar, crude oil, wind and hydroelectric icons.' %}

As work progressed I continued to design icons for the interface, creating a range of labels and controls.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='iconography_web.png' caption='Partial selection of the custom icons designed for the interface labels and controls.' %}
6. Color

While the design system works perfectly in black and white setting, color provides an additional layer of identity and function to the visual language. Associating color with energy types lessens the cognitive load on the user and makes it easier to find and categorize content.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='' outer_width='' background_color='' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='pantone.png' %}

In making the selection I focused on choosing color that reproduced consistently across print and digital formats. Pantone ‘Color Bridge’ served as a good starting point with its wide range of spot colors, sRGB and HEX values, and their CMYK alternatives.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='color.png' caption='Examples of color and energy source pairs. Depending on the context, color can can be applied to any combination of the icon, ray or name of the energy source.' %}
7. Adaptive Energy Branding

The combination of dynamic logo, icon and label is appropriate for major headings. However, depending on the media size or heading hierarchy, this implementation can become unwieldy. Building on the adaptive wordmark, typography and labeling system, energy branding is designed to readjust in responsive interface workflows.

{% include system/figure_centered.html art_direction='left_align' background='see_through ' outer_width='content_media_outerframe' background_color='gray' inner_width='feed_wrap' figure='energy_adaptive.png' caption='Alternatively, energy labels and icons can be used on their own. In the case of a ‘typographic-only’ approach the name of the energy source would be the one taking on the color. ' %}

At this stage the design system was largely completed and my work shifted towards developing how the interface looks and functions. A change occurred in Blue Terminal’s focus as well, as they moved from fundraising to recruiting software engineers and promoting the project.

To help this new direction I updated the original website with new design and functionality. It integrated the newly developed visual language, previewed images of the interface, detailed the project and provided a place to host privately-accessible functionality demos.

{% include system/figure_centered.html art_direction='' background='' outer_width='' inner_width='' figure='website_update.jpg' caption='Updated version of the website. Note the adaptive wordmark, prominent sign-up form and Proxima Nova typography. Images used were of various feature demos hosted on the site.' %}

Virtually all of the design was done in browser, letting me work on interactions and responsive layouts on the fly, and integrating cross-browser testing into the development process.

Additionally this ‘in-browser’ workflow gave Blue Terminal access to live interface prototypes. This enabled easy back and forth between us and provided the team with functional demos they used in engineering RFP’s and product presentations.

{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Site Development' %}

Culture Code won the RFP to build the Blue Terminal’s back-end and article aggregation engine. From that point on we worked together to develop and launch the first version of the site.

We started out by assessing how to implement the project under the available budget and timeframe. This resulted in a roadmap, focused on introducing functionality in sets of compatible features — in the first stage we built out the article feed, user dashboards and developed core interface elements.

1. Article Feed

The design of the feed brings structure and visual style to Blue Terminal’s broad, information-dense content aggregation. Prominent search and an eye-catching ‘featured’ section kick-off the page, providing a quick way to find information and promoting headline articles.

{% include system/figure_flex.html art_direction='left_align' background=' dark' inner_width='content_media_center' figure='article_feed_page.jpg' caption='Snapshot of the article feed — showing the prominent search, art-directed content and interactive article cards.' %}

Below the ‘featured’ section PJAX-driven filters (energy source, time range and article categories) enable users to instantly focus the feed on what they are looking for. Feed content itself is fully art-directable, with 3 levels of emphasis to highlight the article cards.

Additionally, these cards are interactive (further detail and preview in the next section), rotating to show saving/sharing controls and extra information on the back of the card.

2. 'Full-Article' Pages

‘Full-article’ page organizes the content of the aggregated articles into a structured layout. This gets the highly-varied, original source formatting out of the way and lets users quickly find the information they need.

{% include system/figure_flex.html art_direction='left_align' background=' dark' inner_width='content_media_center' figure='full_article_page.jpg' caption='‘Full-article’ page provides summary and analysis, source info, sharing/bookmarking tools and a selection of related articles.' %}
3. User Dashboards

The third major part of the initial build is the user profile/personal dashboard page. It stores bookmarked articles, provides content submission tools and creates a foundation for a future research network.

{% include system/figure_flex.html art_direction='left_align' background=' dark' inner_width='content_media_center' figure='profile_page.jpg' caption='Profile page collects saved articles, and creates a starting point for planned research network.' %}
{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Interface Details' %}

Blue Terminal has a robust content categorization system, making tag-based search a powerful tool for finding the exact information user needs. The design of the search field emphasizes this through prominent page placement, bold typography and stricking background images.

Photos are selected from a curated list to match the theme of the ‘featured’ content, which follows the search/navigation and showcases top articles.

{% include work/blue_terminal/navigation.html %}

Article feed is built on a simple, 3-column grid with flexible width columns and fixed height rows. When viewport size changes the grid flexes until a breaking point is reached (based on line length), at which point it reflows into a more suitable layout (1, 2 or 3 columns).

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='' background_color='' outer_width='' inner_width='content_wrap' figure='grid.png' %}

Individual article cards make up the feed. Similar to the ‘full-page’ template they are designed to process information from the aggregated content into a clear, structured presentation.

Because aggregated content, such as titles or abstracts, can get very long, a rotating interaction was developed — it creates room for the content without increasing the overall size of the card. When activated the card flips around to show additional information and sharing/saving controls.

There is a selection of layouts built into the interface that allow Blue Terminal to feature and emphasize articles in the feed.

There are both text and image based templates, as well as tools for adding custom images. Article cards can span 1, 2 or 3 columns [for varying amount of emphasis] and, as with the rest of the interface, all layouts are responsive.
