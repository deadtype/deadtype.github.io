Lori Lees-Stout is an artist in Vancouver, BC — I designed a personal identity for her, using Lori’s paintings to create a series of miniature art prints. Clean typographic design, combined with high-quality printing and card stock, resulted in a set of cards, which Lori uses for personal communication and self-promotion.

{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Stock + Typography' %}

To achieve solid, canvas-like prints I used a heavy, uncoated stock as a base. The paper is bright white with a textured finish which lets Lori’s artwork stand-out and gives the cards a firm, tactile feel.

{% include system/figure_flex.html background='' art_direction='left_align' background_color='' outer_width='' inner_width='content_media_center' figure='texture_typography.jpg' caption='Close-up of the information-focused card side, showing detail of the texture and Garamond typography.' %}

Adobe Garamond underscores the art [without stealing attention from it] and gives the cards a subtle, ‘art-gallery’ styled feel.

{% include system/figure_centered.html background='see_through ' background_color='gray' outer_width='content_media_center' inner_width='content_alignto_left_margin' figure='garamond.png' caption='Italic is used to emphasize titles while Roman sets the content.' %}
{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Artwork' %}

For the initial run of prints we chose a set of 5 paintings out of Lori’s favorite works to showcase her range of drawing styles and striking use of color. White border frames the images, focusing viewers on the art.

Note the readjustment of text between the portrait and landscape orientations, allowing for a customized use of space to set the artwork in.
{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Custom Template' %}

Building on the design, I created a editable template for Lori, letting her easily add new artwork in future. Because portrait and landscape formats require subtle changes to the layout of text and images, the template includes both versions, as well as simple guidelines on how to apply them.

{% include system/figure_centered.html inner_width='' background_color='' outer_width='' figure='portrait.jpg' %} {% include system/figure_centered.html inner_width='' background_color='' outer_width='' figure='landscape.jpg' %}
Two versions of the information-focused side of the card — used selectively for portrait or landscape prints.
{% include system/divider_label_param.html param='Card Use' %}

Lori uses the prints as both personal business cards and samples of her art — giving them out in galleries and at exhibitions.

The ‘miniature painting’ design of the cards helps Lori creatively promote her work. Placing them alongside corresponding full-sized paintings, gives gallery visitors a small version to keep — while arranging prints along hallways or walls creates striking ‘micro-exhibitions’.
